Falco - A Tribute Page

The first white rapper

Short Info:

Falco, was an Austrian singer and musician. He had several international hits, including "Der Kommissar" (1981), "Rock Me Amadeus","Vienna Calling", "Jeanny", "The Sound of Musik", "Coming Home (Jeanny Part II, One Year Later)", and posthumously "Out of the Dark".
"Rock Me Amadeus" reached No. 1 on the Billboard charts in 1986, making Falco the only artist in history to score a number-one hit with a German language song in the United States. According to his estate, he has sold 20 million albums and 40 million singles, which makes him the best-selling Austrian singer of all time.
Also notable was his very early creation of a successful non-English language rap music song ("Der Kommissar"), recorded in December 1981.

Dates and Data:

19.02.1957 Born in Vienna as Johann Hoelzel.
1963 Beginns attending elementary school.
1967 Beginns attending the "Rainergymnasium" in Vienna's 5th district "Margareten".
1973 Attends the Vienna Conservatoire
1974 8 months of compulsory military service.
Late 70s Plays bass guitar in a number of bands under various pseudonyms, including "John Hudson" and "John DiFalco"
1981 Gets signed by manager Robert Ponger
1981 "Der Kommissar" gets released and becomes a number-one success in Germany, France, Italy, Spain and Japan as well as topping the charts in Austria and the Netherlands.
1986 Collaboration with producers Rob and Ferdi Bolland beginns.
1986 "Rock Me Amadeus" gets released and reaches Billboard Hot 100 position 1.
Early 90s Falco permanently moves to the Dominican Republic
1992 Comeback album "Nachtflug" gets released but fails to meet expectations.
1995 - 1998 Work on Falco's last album
6 February 1998 Killed in a car accident
27 February 1998 His last album "Out Of the Dark" gets released posthumously.
